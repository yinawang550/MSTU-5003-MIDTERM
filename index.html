Why do you need to tell STAR stories in the Interview...

  • PROVE you’ve actually had the experiences that show you can handle the job.
  • SHOW how you approach and solve problems, as well as how you apply certain skills.
  • STAR makes it easy for interviewers to remember you and compare you with others.


Situation:

This is the backstory: the who, what, where, and when. This can show what sorts of situations you've been in, as well as the limitations of the situation

Example:

"When I was working as a UX Designer at XYZ International, there was a situation where I had to design a brand new Web page in less than five hours.."

Task:

The task portion of the STAR interview stems from the previous question. What was your exact part to play in this situation? What was your assigned role?

Examples:

"On the project, I was assigned to design graphics for the new Web page. I saw it as an opportunity which would help our customers to see the value in our product."


Action:

What were the steps you took to solve this challenge? How did you think about and overcome the problems?

*Essential part of your STAR:

You should explain your actions step-by-step in a high level of detail to make the story feel credible. This can help paint a picture of the task for your interviewer and showcase your contributions to it.

Result:

Sum up the tangible results of your work. For instance, how were things better off because of what you did? What lessons did you learn?

Examples:

"My efforts have brought in 10 percent more Web traffic, resulting in more customers." *Detail is highly necessary in the results.


  Tips: You need to use as many quantifiable metrics and specifics as you can to prove that you had an impact. Such as percentages, before and after comparisons, and even client feedback are all helpful to prove the value of your work.
